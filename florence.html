Famous Florence Attractions

Piazzale Michelangiolo

Piazzale Michelangiolo is a square that is a known tourist spot because of its wonderful skyline view. From the Piazzale Michelangiolo you're able to see all of Florence. Most of the tourists visit during sunset because the gold glow over the city looks amazing. The square was built in 1869 while Florence was the capital of Italy. During this time period Florence was going to an urban renewal. This Piazzale was designed by Giuseppe Poggi. The square was also built for Michelangelo and because of this the designer has a bronze statue made for him in the center.








Uffizi Palace and Gallery

The Uffizi gallery is on the first and second floor in the Historic Center of Florence. This gallery is famous because of its collections of ancient paintings and sculptures. These pieces are from the middle ages and the modern age. It is primarily known for its 14th century Renaissance art. It has masterpieces from Giotto, Leonardo, Michelangelo, Simone Martini, Raffaello, and more. The first part of the museum was built on the second floor and was made possible by Francesco I, Grand Duke.








Santa Croce

The Santa Croce is the biggest Franciscan church in the world. It is most famous for its 16 chapels. This builded was constructed to replace an older building in 1294. It's believed it was paid for by some of the wealthiest families in the city. The church has a lot of art pieces inside. The entire inside of the church is filled top to bottom with decorated walls. There are also monuments from 500 years ago for royalty and artists.








Ponte Vecchio

The Ponte Vecchio is a bridge built in 1345 for the Florence Republican Government because the one before it was destroyed due to a flood. It was built between 1339 and 1346. It is built of the Arno River and was used as a system of defence. Over time the government started selling parts of the bridge to be used as a shoping centre. Present day it is used for jewlery shops, boutiques shops, and a wonderful view. It is also the only bridge spared from World War 2.

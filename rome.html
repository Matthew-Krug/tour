The Capital: Rome

The Colosseum

The Colosseum is a renowned ancient amphitheater located in the heart of Rome, Italy. It was built in 80 AD during the reign of Emperor Titus Flavius, and it is considered one of the greatest architectural wonders of the ancient world. The amphitheater was constructed from travertine limestone, tuff, and brick, and it was designed to seat over 50,000 spectators. The Colosseum was used for gladiatorial contests, animal hunts, and public spectacles for over 400 years. The games were often bloody and brutal, and it is believed that tens of thousands of people and animals were killed in the arena. The Colosseum was also used for other events such as mock sea battles and public executions. The Colosseum has undergone numerous changes and renovations throughout the centuries, including damage from earthquakes, fires, and looting. However, it has remained a prominent symbol of Rome's rich history and cultural heritage.

In 1980, the Colosseum was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and it attracts millions of visitors each year. Today, visitors to the Colosseum can take guided tours to learn about its history and significance, as well as explore the underground chambers and tunnels where gladiators and animals were kept before their battles. The Colosseum also offers stunning views of the surrounding Roman Forum and Palatine Hill. Despite its brutal past, the Colosseum remains a popular tourist destination and a testament to the engineering and architectural achievements of ancient Rome. It serves as a reminder of the city's rich and complex history, and continues to inspire awe and wonder in those who visit.


St Peter's Basilica

St. Peter's Basilica is a massive church located in Vatican City, Rome. It is one of the most iconic buildings in the world and is considered the most significant church in Catholicism. The basilica was designed by Michelangelo, Donato Bramante, Carlo Maderno, and Gian Lorenzo Bernini, among other prominent architects. Construction of the basilica began in 1506 and took over 120 years to complete. The dome of St. Peter's Basilica, designed by Michelangelo, is 448 feet tall and can be seen from all over Rome. Visitors can climb up to the top of the dome for a stunning panoramic view of the city. The basilica is also home to several tombs of popes and other significant figures, including St. Peter himself. In addition to being a religious site, St. Peter's Basilica is also a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year who come to marvel at its stunning architecture and art.

The Pantheon

The Pantheon is a magnificent ancient Roman temple located in the heart of Rome, Italy. It was originally built as a temple to all the gods of ancient Rome, but it has been used for various purposes over the years, including as a church and a tomb. The Pantheon was built between 118 and 125 AD, during the reign of the Roman Emperor Hadrian. It was designed by the architect Apollodorus of Damascus and is renowned for its massive dome, which is made of concrete and is still one of the largest unsupported domes in the world. The interior of the Pantheon is equally impressive, with its stunning marble floors, intricate columns, and intricate mosaics. The main feature of the interior is the oculus, a circular opening in the dome that allows light to filter into the temple and creates a striking effect.

One of the most fascinating aspects of the Pantheon is its durability. Despite being over 1,800 years old, the building is still standing and has survived earthquakes, fires, and countless wars. In addition to its architectural and historical significance, the Pantheon has also played a crucial role in the religious and cultural life of Rome. It is still used as a church, and many important figures, including the artist Raphael and several Italian kings, are buried in its walls. Today, the Pantheon remains one of the most visited tourist attractions in Rome, attracting millions of visitors every year who come to marvel at its ancient beauty and learn about its rich history.

St. Peter's Square

St. Peter's Square is located in Vatican City, Rome, Italy, and is one of the most famous public spaces in the world. It was designed by Gian Lorenzo Bernini in the 17th century as a grand entrance to St. Peter's Basilica. The square is surrounded by massive colonnades with 284 columns and 88 pilasters, and is large enough to accommodate tens of thousands of people for papal events.

At the center of the square stands an Egyptian obelisk that was brought to Rome in the 1st century. The square is also home to two fountains, one on either side of the obelisk, which were designed by Carlo Maderno and Bernini. St. Peter's Square is a popular tourist attraction and a place of great significance for Catholics around the world, as it is where the Pope delivers his public addresses and blessings. The square is also the site of many historic events, including the election of new popes and the funerals of past popes.
